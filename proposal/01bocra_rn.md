## What BOCRA Is

The Botswana Communications Regulatory Authority (BOCRA) is a government agency established under the Communications Regulatory Authority Act of 2012. It is mandated to regulate all matters related to telecommunications, postal services, internet, ICTs, radio communications and broadcasting in Botswana — in the public interest.

BOCRA is the single most important digital infrastructure regulator in Botswana. Every internet service provider, every broadcaster, every mobile network operator, every postal service and every domain registered under .BW falls under its authority.

---

## Services BOCRA Currently Offers

### 1. Licensing
BOCRA processes applications for and issues licences, permits, permissions, concessions and authorities for all regulated sectors. Licence types include:
- Aircraft Radio
- Amateur Radio
- Broadcasting (Community, Commercial, Public)
- Cellular / Mobile Networks
- Satellite Communications
- Value Added Network Services (VANS)
- Internet Service Providers
- Postal Services

### 2. Complaints Handling
BOCRA handles complaints from citizens against licensed service providers. According to their mandate, complaints should be attended to within two days of receipt. For complex complaints that may take longer, the complainant is supposed to be kept informed about progress.

### 3. Domain Registry (.BW)
BOCRA manages the .BW country-code top-level domain as a national resource. It acts as trustee for all .BW second-level domains including co.bw, org.bw, net.bw and gov.bw.

### 4. Consumer Protection
BOCRA monitors quality of service provided by licensees and educates citizens about their rights as consumers of telecommunications, internet and broadcasting services.

### 5. Type Approval
BOCRA approves all communications devices and equipment used in Botswana — from mobile phones to radio transmitters.

### 6. Cybersecurity
Under the Electronic Communications and Transaction Act, BOCRA has cybersecurity advisory responsibilities for the national digital infrastructure.

### 7. Postal Regulation
BOCRA regulates Botswana Post and other postal service operators.

## What Is Broken — The Critical Findings

### Finding 1: No Online Complaint Submission (Most Critical)
This is the single most damaging failure of the current platform.

Complaints may only be brought to BOCRA by **post, hand delivery, email or fax.**

A citizen in 2026 with a billing dispute against Mascom or BTC must either:
- Physically walk into BOCRA's offices in Gaborone (Private Bag 00495, Independence Avenue)
- Send a fax to +267 3957976
- Write an email with no structured form, no acknowledgement, and no tracking

There is no online complaint submission form. There is no reference number system. There is no way for a citizen to know if their complaint was received, being investigated, or resolved. This is the core failure our solution addresses.

### Finding 2: No Complaint Tracking System
Even if a citizen submits a complaint by email, there is no portal where they can check its status. They must call or visit the offices to follow up — defeating the purpose of digital engagement entirely.

### Finding 3: No Online Licensing Portal
Licensees — the companies that pay BOCRA fees and keep its operations funded — cannot apply for or renew licences online. Every application is a physical paper process. This is a significant burden on the business community.

### Finding 4: No Self-Service for Any Stakeholder
There is no self-service capability for any user group:
- Citizens cannot submit complaints
- Licensees cannot apply or renew
- The public cannot search regulatory documents easily
- BOCRA staff have no internal dashboard for managing incoming work

### Finding 5: No Real-Time Data or Analytics
BOCRA has no public-facing dashboard showing regulatory activity — number of complaints received, resolved, pending. No transparency into how the regulator is performing. This reduces public trust.

### Finding 6: No Mobile-Responsive Design
The current website is not optimised for mobile devices. Given that the majority of Batswana access the internet via mobile phone, this is a critical accessibility failure.

### Finding 7: Fragmented Information Architecture
Publications, regulations, acts, consumer guides and licensing information are not integrated or easily searchable. Users must know what they are looking for before they can find it.
